## ğŸ” System Design & Flow

### 1. Seawater Intake
- Seawater is drawn directly from the ocean into a **pre-filtration unit** to remove sediments and organic matter.
- The intake relies on **gravity-fed channels** to reduce pumping energy.

### 2. Data Center Cooling
- Seawater flows through **heat exchangers** in the data center to absorb thermal energy from servers.
- The heated water is not wasted â€” instead, it becomes a **feedstock for vapor generation**.

### 3. Vaporization & Tunnel Transfer
- Heated seawater is channeled into **cemented solar-assisted tunnels** designed to promote controlled evaporation.
- The tunnels are powered by **solar panels**, maintaining temperature and airflow to continue vaporization.
- As water vapor moves through the tunnels, it undergoes **multi-stage condensation** and **salt extraction**.

### 4. Salt & Mineral Extraction
- Along the tunnel, **collection chambers** and **filter membranes** separate minerals and salts at different saturation levels.
- Extracted materials (like sodium chloride, magnesium, lithium, etc.) are processed or sold commercially.
- Remaining brine, now stripped of useful minerals, is treated before discharge.

### 5. Condensation & Secondary Pond
- Water vapor condenses into liquid form within the **secondary cooling pond**.
- This pond acts as a **natural condenser**, using ambient air and gravity to re-liquidify vapor.
- Collected water is then sent for **desalination** and purification.

### 6. Desalination & Freshwater Output
- The condensed water undergoes **membrane-based desalination** (such as RO or solar distillation) to produce **drinkable water**.
- This process is partially powered by solar energy and partially by **recovered heat** from the data center.
- Output: **Freshwater + commercial salt + minerals.**

### 7. Salt Return & Ocean Restoration
- After useful minerals are extracted, remaining salts and trace elements are **safely returned to the ocean**.
- This maintains the **natural mineral balance** of marine ecosystems.
